**Databricks — "Lakeflow Realtime" announcement:** At Databricks Summit preview day, Databricks announced Lakeflow Realtime, extending Delta Live Tables into low-latency streaming. Feature overlap with DataStream is material but Databricks remains platform-locked and weak on multi-cloud lineage. Jordan's read: near-term headwind for messaging to Databricks-native shops, but strengthens the "neutral, multi-cloud, freshness-SLA" wedge for the segment DataStream actually wins. Series A deck needs an updated competitive slide before May outreach begins.

**Confluent move — "Stream Governance 2.0":** Confluent shipped expanded data lineage and schema governance tooling. Positioned at Kafka-heavy enterprises. DataStream's counter-positioning: feature freshness SLAs and ML-feature-specific semantics are not in Confluent's scope. Yuki should publish a technical blog contrasting "pipeline lineage" (Confluent) vs. "feature lineage" (DataStream) within two weeks.

**Snowflake — Openflow pricing cut:** Snowflake announced a 30% list-price reduction on Openflow streaming ingestion. Aggressive defense against Databricks. Indirect DataStream impact: compresses the price point for adjacent streaming-ingest tooling. Jordan should confirm with Yuki that DataStream's per-event pricing stays competitive — this is also the pricing-model concern flagged in index.md risks.

**Customer signal:** Two large fintech ML teams (Ramp and Mercury, per LinkedIn posts from platform engineers) publicly discussed "feature freshness SLAs" as a Q2 priority. Jordan flagging to Yuki for outbound; both fit the enterprise reference customer profile needed for Series A.

**Key-hire signal:** Ex-Pinecone growth lead Marco Liu posted a "first week at DataStream" LinkedIn update. Strong PLG/content hire; helps the Series A narrative on GTM readiness.
